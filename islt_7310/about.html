I love history and delving into the past. My natural curiosity allows me to use the past to interpret the present and I’m always mindful of how the present will look when it becomes the past. I enjoy researching family traditions and learning how life was for the people who came before me. Some traditions are beautiful and I love to see them carry on, while some are harmful that should be left in the past. We must break generational curses that are harmful and do not serve our goals. One of the most impactful ways to do this is by increasing financial literacy in your family or community. My passion is to help anyone who is “bad” with money learn how to manage their time and resources more effectively.
